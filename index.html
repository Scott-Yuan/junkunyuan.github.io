Biography [] []

I am currently a third-year Ph.D. student at the College of Computer Science and Technology, Zhejiang University, supervised by Prof. Kun Kuang, Prof. Lanfen Lin, and Prof. Fei Wu.

I am focusing on improving the stability and generalization of machine learning in the dynamic, open, and non-stationary real world. My current research interests include domain generalization, domain adaptation, and causal inference.

Collaborative Semantic Aggregation and Calibration for Separated Domain Generalization.

Junkun Yuan, Xu Ma, Defang Chen, Kun Kuang*, Fei Wu, Lanfen Lin.

arXiv preprint, 2021 (under review)

Abstract: We investigate separated domain generalization task where the source datasets are separated and can only be used locally for privacy protection. To address this challenging task, we propose a novel solution with privacy preserving Collaborative Semantic Aggregation and Calibration (CSAC), which is composed of local semantic acquisition, data-free semantic aggregation, and cross-layer semantic calibration.

Domain-Specific Bias Filtering for Single Labeled Domain Generalization.

Junkun Yuan#, Xu Ma#, Defang Chen, Kun Kuang*, Fei Wu, Lanfen Lin.

arXiv preprint, 2021 (under review)

Abstract: To reduce reliance on expensive data annotation, we investigate Single Labeled Domain Generalization (SLDG) task. We propose Domain-Specific Bias Filtering (DSBF) for this challenging task which initializes a discriminative model with labeled source data and filters out its domain-specific bias with unlabeled source data.

Auto IV: Counterfactual Prediction via Automatic Instrumental Variable Decomposition.

Junkun Yuan, Anpeng Wu, Kun Kuang*, Bo Li, Runze Wu, Fei Wu, Lanfen Lin.

ACM Transactions on Knowledge Discovery from Data (TKDD), 2021 (CCF-B, IF: 2.713)

Abstract: Instrumental Variable (IV) is a powerful tool for causal inference, but it is hard to find/pre-define valid IVs. Inspired by recent progress in mutual information estimation, we propose a novel Automatic Instrumental Variable decomposition (AutoIV) algorithm to automatically generate representations serving the role of IVs from observed variables (IV candidates) through mutual information constraints under an adversarial game.

Subgraph Networks with Application to Structural Feature Space Expansion.

Qi Xuan*, Jinhuan Wang, Minghao Zhao, Junkun Yuan, Chenbo Fu, Zhongyuan Ruan*, Guanrong Chen.

IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering (TKDE) , 2021 (CCF-A, IF: 6.977)

Abstract: We introduce a new concept of subgraph network (SGN) and design algorithms to construct 1st-order and 2nd-order SGNs, which can be easily extended to build higher-order ones. SGNs are used to obtain a series of handcrafted structural features which, together with the features automatically extracted by using some advanced methods, provide complementary features to the extracted features, improving network classification performance.

Learning Decomposed Representations for Treatment Effect Estimation.

Anpeng Wu#, Junkun Yuan#, Kun Kuang*, Bo Li, Pan Zhou, Jianrong Tao, Qiang Zhu, Yueting Zhuang, Fei Wu.

arXiv preprint, 2020 (under review)

Abstract: By modeling causal relations among observed pre-treatment variables, treatment variables, and outcome variables, we propose a learning framework to i) separate confounders by learning decomposed representations of both confounders and non-confounders, ii) balance confounders with sample re-weighting technique, and iii) estimate the treatment effect in observational studies via counterfactual prediction.

Black-box Adversarial Attacks Against Deep Learning Based Malware Binaries Detection with GAN.

Junkun Yuan, Shaofang Zhou, Lanfen Lin*, Feng Wang, Jia Cui.

European Conference on Artificial Intelligence (ECAI) , 2020 (CCF-B)

Abstract: The existing attack methods for malware detection are based on manual features or in white-box setting, which is not practical. We propose a novel black-box attack framework called GAPGAN, which Generates Adversarial Payloads (padding bytes) with generative adversarial networks (GANs). We map input discrete malware binaries to continuous space, and then feed it to a generator to generate adversarial payloads. The payloads are appended to the original binaries to craft an adversarial sample while preserving its functionality.

Shaofang Zhou, Lanfen Lin*, Junkun Yuan, Feng Wang, Zhaoting Ling, Jia Cui.
